Hi~ I am a PhD student in computational neuroscience working at the interface of artificial intelligence and systems neuroscience. My research develops large-scale neural encoding models for the mouse visual cortex, with an emphasis on biologically constrained architectures that can serve not only as predictors of population activity, but also as computational substrates for circuit-level analysis.

A central motivation of my work is the reciprocal exchange between AI and neuroscience: scalable deep models provide increasingly expressive tools for modeling neural systems, while biological circuits offer structural priors and learning principles that can inform more robust and interpretable AI. In the long term, I aim to build foundation-scale models that function as experimentally grounded digital twins of cortex and support in-silico dissection of neural computation.

Research Interests

Foundation models for cortical neural encoding

I develop large-scale neural encoding models that predict population activity in the mouse visual cortex across animals, sessions, stimuli, and recording modalities. The objective is generalization: shared representations that transfer to new experimental contexts and remain effective in low-data regimes where session-specific models are underconstrained.

The brain as a prior for deep models

I study how anatomical and functional organization can be incorporated into deep networks as explicit inductive biases. Rather than treating cortical structure as incidental detail, this direction asks whether constraints derived from biological circuits can improve robustness, neural alignment, and interpretability beyond what unconstrained scaling provides.

Understanding brain computation through biologically constrained models

I use biologically constrained encoding models as in-silico testbeds for studying cortical computation. Beyond predictive performance, such models support targeted perturbations and analyses of candidate circuit motifs, yielding mechanistic hypotheses about visual cortex that can be tested experimentally.
